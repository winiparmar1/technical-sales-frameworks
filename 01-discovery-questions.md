# Discovery Questions — Challenger Style

> Questions that teach, reframe, and surface pain the buyer hasn't named yet.

The goal is not to qualify. The goal is to make the buyer see their situation differently before you've said a word about your product.

---

## Opening — earn the right to challenge

**Q01 — The productivity walk**
> "Walk me through how a new developer gets fully productive in your current environment — from day one to shipping to prod."

*Intent: expose the invisible tax of toolchain debt. Most orgs have 6+ manual steps baked in. Let them narrate the pain before you name it.*

---

**Q02 — The 2am question**
> "When something breaks in production at 2am — what does your on-call rotation actually touch to triage it?"

*Intent: surface infrastructure sprawl. Their answer tells you exactly where complexity lives and who owns it.*

---

**Q03 — The competitive reframe**
> "If I told you that your top three competitors are standardizing their QA and release cycles on a unified platform — how would that change your thinking?"

*Classic Challenger move. You're introducing competitive urgency without making a product claim. Watch for the posture shift.*

---

## Economic buyer — CFO / VP Engineering

**Q04 — The cloud spend question**
> "How does your engineering org currently report cloud spend back to finance — by team, by project, or as one consolidated line?"

*Intent: probe cost visibility. If they can't attribute cloud spend to product lines, they can't defend or optimize it. You become the translation layer.*

---

**Q05 — The delay question**
> "What does a two-week release delay actually cost you — in engineering time, in lost feature velocity, in customer commitments?"

*Force quantification. Most buyers have never said the number out loud. Once they do, your ROI story writes itself.*

---

**Q06 — The growth question**
> "If your SaaS renewal is tied to usage growth — what happens to your infra costs if you 3x your active users next year?"

*Scales the consequence. SaaS growth is not free — it has infrastructure cost implications they may not have modeled. You're ahead of their CFO.*

---

## Technical buyer — Engineering lead / DevOps

**Q07 — The architecture question**
> "Are you running multi-cloud today, or consolidating? And is that a strategy or something that just happened organically?"

*Tells you whether this is deliberate architecture or accumulated technical debt. Both create urgency — just different kinds.*

---

**Q08 — The tribal knowledge question**
> "How much of your current pipeline is automated versus still relying on tribal knowledge to execute?"

*Surfaces bus-factor risk. Engineering leads hate admitting this is still a problem. Naming it creates trust.*
