# Champion Map — Multi-Threading Done Right

> The warm intro is not a champion. Access is not influence. Know the difference.

---

## The personas in a cloud infra + SaaS deal

| Persona | What they care about | How to activate them |
|---|---|---|
| **VP Engineering** | Velocity, reliability, team retention — not cost | Show how your platform removes the work they hate managing |
| **DevOps / Platform lead** | Stability, rollback, integrations — will this break what we have? | Deep technical credibility. Speak their language or earn their silence. |
| **QA / Test lead** | Coverage, speed, not babysitting pipelines at 11pm | Give them a win they can show their manager. They'll champion you upward. |
| **CFO / Finance** | Unit economics — cost per deploy, cloud bill growth | Translate engineering outcomes into dollar figures. Be their translator. |
| **CTO / CISO** | Strategic fit, security posture, vendor consolidation | One clear narrative: this reduces complexity at scale. Don't feature-dump. |

---

## The champion test — three questions

A real champion has access, influence, and skin in the game. Test for all three.

**Test 1 — access**
> "Would you be willing to set up a call between me and your VP of Engineering before the POC readout?"

A real champion opens doors. If they hesitate, you have a fan — not a champion.

---

**Test 2 — skin in the game**
> "What's your personal stake in this decision going well?"

Champions have something to win or lose. If they can't answer this, they won't fight for you internally.

---

**Test 3 — internal narrative**
> "If I'm not in the room when this gets debated internally — what's your argument for us?"

You're coaching them and testing them at once. Their answer is your deal review. If it's weak, you have work to do.

---

## Multi-threading rule

Never rely on a single thread. Map at least three active contacts across two levels of seniority. If one goes dark, the deal shouldn't die with them.

> "Who else on your team has a stake in how this decision goes?"

Ask it early. Ask it often. It's not aggressive — it's good selling.
