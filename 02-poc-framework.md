# POC Framework — Structure Wins That Stick

> A POC without defined success criteria is just a product demo with a longer timeline.

---

## The three rules before you start

1. **Lock success criteria in writing on day one.** "Works well" is not a criterion. "Reduces manual testing effort by 30% across two workflows" is.
2. **Get the economic buyer committed to the readout before the POC begins.** If they won't commit, your POC is theatre.
3. **Scope for a win, not for impressiveness.** Choose the use case most likely to show a clear, measurable delta. Early momentum beats big ambition.

---

## Before the POC

| Action | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Define success criteria jointly | Prevents goalposts from moving mid-eval |
| Confirm who's in the readout room | If the EB won't commit now, they won't show up |
| Choose 3 use cases max | More than 3 = scope creep = no decision |
| Set a check-in cadence | Silence mid-POC = dying deal |

**Ask before you start:**
> "If we hit these three outcomes, is there anything that would prevent you from moving forward?"

Their answer tells you what the real blockers are before you've spent three weeks proving value.

---

## During the POC

**Week 1 — stay close**
- Daily check-in with your champion
- Track who is actively logging in vs who isn't — the non-users are your detractors
- Fix blockers fast. A slow POC is a dying deal.

**Week 2 — surface an early win**
- Mid-point readout with the technical buyer
- Send a written summary: *"Here's what we've seen so far and what we're targeting for the readout."*
- Even a small early win creates momentum — and momentum is a selling tool

---

## The readout

Lead with their metric. Not your feature list.

> "You wanted 30% reduction in manual testing effort — we hit 41% across the first two workflows."

Then stop talking. Let the silence after the number work for you.

After the readout, ask directly:
> "Based on what you've seen — what would it take to move forward?"

Don't summarize. Don't feature-dump. Let them close themselves.
